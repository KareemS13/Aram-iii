Why does the size change?
Lake Sevan's surface area shifts with the seasons — it's typically largest in late summer after snowmelt fills it, and smallest in winter. On top of that, the lake shrank dramatically between 1998 and 2006 due to water extraction for irrigation and hydropower, then partially recovered after government protections were introduced.

How is this forecast made?
The predictions use a statistical model (SARIMA) trained on 18 years of satellite data from 2007 to present — the period after the lake stabilised. It learns the lake's typical seasonal pattern and projects it forward. The further out the forecast, the wider the uncertainty band. This is a statistical model only — it doesn't know about future rainfall, policy changes, or droughts.
